Forty years of building futures.

Our Legacy

From a small Clifton classroom in 1983 to four generations of alumni.

Playway Grammar opened its doors in 1983 with a simple promise: combine the academic standards parents wanted with the joyful childhood every kid deserves. Four decades later, we still measure ourselves by that same promise.

The proof is in the families. Parents who studied here in the 80s and 90s now bring their own children. Some now bring their grandchildren. That kind of trust isn't built in a year — it's earned, one school day at a time.

What We Stand For

Three principles, forty years strong.

These aren't slogans we put on a wall. They're the lens we make every decision through.

A

Academic excellence

Strong reading, sharp maths, real science. We don't compromise on the basics — and we don't apologise for it either.

B

Character first

Honesty, respect, perseverance. The kind of character that shows up long after the report card is forgotten.

C

Joyful childhood

Children should run, sing, paint, and laugh every day at school. Joy isn't the opposite of standards — it's how standards stick.
